The RFC Index is https://tools.ietf.org/. To look at RFC 777, go to https://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc777.

1) What was the first RFC number for ICMP? What is its current status?
RFC 777, deprecated by RFC0792

2) What is the current RFC number of ICMP?
RFC 792

3) What RFCs update the RFC you mentioned in your answer to (2)?
RFC 950, 4884, 6633, 6918

4) How long is the checksum field in ICMPv4?
16 bits

5) What is the type of a Source Quench Message?
Type 4

6) May a host send ICMP Source Quench Messages? Provide a quote supporting your answer.
Some students may say yes, because this is how it was defined in an early RFC of ICMP. However, in RFC6633 this was changed:
"A host MUST NOT send ICMP Source Quench Messages, if a source quench message is received, the IP layer MAY silently discard it."
Source: "tools.ietf.org/html/rfc6633"
